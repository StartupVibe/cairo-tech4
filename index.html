Dwelding test

evaluates the quality of a weld and can be performed using non-destructive testing (NDT), which inspects the weld without damaging it, or destructive testing (DT), which involves breaking or deforming the weld to assess its properties. NDT methods include visual inspection and using tools like dye penetrants to find surface or internal defects, while DT methods involve tests like bend tests to check ductility or tensile tests to determine strength

07

Sandblasting

is a surface preparation process that uses compressed air to propel abrasive materials at high velocity to clean, smooth, or roughen a hard surface. It is used to remove contaminants like rust or old paint, to prepare a surface for a new coating, or to create a decorative finish. Different abrasive materials, not just sand, are used for various purposes and surfaces, though using silica sand is hazardous and strictly regulated due to health risks.
